Impression

Materiality | Collective Work

2019, Tokyo

Final Showcase for the Student Collective Keio Fashion Creator, Fashion Design at ESMOD Japon




During the dressmaking process for the fashion show "Impression," I delved into the intricate relationships between fabrics, focusing particularly on the captivating effects of layering.



What did I do?

I began by experimenting with silhouettes using cotton muslin. Through several fittings with models, I gradually incorporated further details with tulle material, making the clothes wearable.






How did I do it?

Keio Fashion Creator is a student-led fashion community in Tokyo that annually hosts a fashion show, as well as regularly collaborating with artists and creative enterprises for fashion shoots and events. It is a student collective consisting of four teams: Creative Direction, Designer, Press, and Model Manager. It brings together students from various universities around Tokyo, including technical and art universities. I took part in the organization of the Keio Fashion Creator 2019 Show while completing an intensive one-year fashion design course at ESMOD (École supérieure des arts et techniques de la mode) Japon, a fashion college.





The final showcase took place at Japan’s national cultural property, designed by the American architect Frank Lloyd Wright, Jiyū Gakuen Myōnichikan, in December 2019, and attracted an audience of more than 500 people in a single day.
