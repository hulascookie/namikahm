Impression

Materiality | Collective Work

2019, Tokyo

Final Showcase for Fashion Design at ESMOD (L'École supérieure des arts et techniques de la mode) Japon





What did I do?

“Layers and Materials” delves into the intricate relationships between fabrics, with a particular focus on the captivating effects of layering. This year-long project dedicated to dress-making and collective organization of the fashion show Keio Fashion Creator 2019 Show. The movements of layered cotton and tulle fabrics aim to show how these materials interacted with light, responding dynamically to movements and introducing a rhythmic quality to the overall appearance. This is an exploration of how clothing evolves and the impressions it can create. Due to the COVID-19 quarantine, it became no longer possible to continue related projects on-site. However, with conversation with the photographer Valeria Aristizabal, the collaborative shooting was realized in September 2023.



How did I do it?

Keio Fashion Creator is a student-led fashion community in Tokyo that annually hosts a fashion show, in addition to its regular collaborations with artists and creative enterprises for fashion shoots and events. Concurrently, I completed an intensive one-year fashion design course at a fashion college ESMOD (École supérieure des arts et techniques de la mode) Japon. The final showcase took place at a Japan’s national cultural property designed by the American architect Frank Lloyd Wright, Jiyū Gakuen Myōnichikan, in December 2019 and attracted an audience of more than 500 people in a single day.
