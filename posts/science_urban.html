Why do you still live in cities?

Applied Anthropology | Writing
2022


Context:

Responses to the Applied Anthropology module GES026 S1: Society and Culture


Description:

This anthropology module at ICU provided insights into the rapid growth of mega-cities in the Global South. It portrayed the increasing fracturing of the urban landscape and infrastructure issues, juxtaposed with the creativity reflected in architectural innovations and new art forms. During the module, I conducted comparative research on city construction processes and socio-economic conditions of urban spheres, such as Dubai, Rio de Janeiro, Lima, and Tokyo. During the process, I studied works by authors such as Tatiana Thieme, Matthew Gandy, Mark Davis, and Rem Koolhaas.

What alternative models of urban development and governance could be proposed? How can cultural expression and social organization symbolically reflect and respond to socio-economic disparities in cities?

Below is the text I wrote in response to weekly readings and classes in the form of a “comment sheet.” The disparity between the economic and cultural senses of the term “cities” that I observed living in Tokyo, and “cities” observed from the global perspectives of population growth and urban infrastructure, is striking. Such notions led me to think that I want to consequently work to build an alternative way of social organization and cultural expression characteristics.
