New Performance Language for Musicians

Project Coordination | Participant Observation

2022, Brussels

European Theatre and Film Institute (ETFI) 's workshop

 

During my internship at ETFI, I contributed to the coordination of 3-day workshops for music students at Koninklijk Conservatorium Brussel (The Royal Conservatory of Brussels). These workshops aimed to raise awareness of physicality and apply theatrical practices in various music genres, particularly classical music and jazz. The following documents key activities conducted during the workshop.

  

Check workshop's details at KCB's website!




How can different disciplines in the performing arts learn from each other?

How do participants' comfort levels and engagement evolve over the course of workshops focused on breaking conventional disciplinary boundaries?




---

Day 1: Concentration Training Games

・Moving objects in a given order collectively without oral communication

・Remembering smells and recognizing people and objects with them

・Quickly arranging participants based on physical characteristics such as hair length and shoe size, without oral communication

 

Day 2: Participant Observation

・Singing and dancing exercises focusing on controlling physicality

・Impromptu performances with participants' specialized instruments

 

Day 3: Improvisation and Spatial Design

・Impromptu performances within proposed themes and frameworks

・Experimentation in spatial design, arranging positions of chairs, music stands, tables, mattresses, etc.

・Solo acting with instruments while incorporating a music stand into the setting (e.g., using a music stand as a prop like a baby, a selfie camera, or an interlocutor).




The following documents feedback from participants:

Saxophonist "Minimal is enough; objects should always be placed with intention. If there is no clarity in their usage, we can remove them."

Pianist "The presence of musicians is part of the performance. It's essential for musicians to be aware of how they behave in different situations. For me, I want to further reflect on how to incorporate the creativity from this workshop into playing classical pieces."




We all have a notion of how we're perceived physically to some extent, but this perception might not be as concrete and precise as that of actors, who professionally study physical behaviors. Interdisciplinarity emerges from identifying gaps in our specializations and complementing them with knowledge from other disciplines.
