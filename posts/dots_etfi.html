New Performance Language for Musicians

Project Coordination | Participant Observation
2022, Brussels


Context

European Theatre and Film Institute (ETFI) 's workshop at Koninklijk Conservatorium Brussel


Check workshop's details at KCB's website!


Description:

How can different disciplines in the performing arts learn from each other? How do participants' comfort levels and engagement evolve over the course of workshops focused on breaking conventional disciplinary boundaries?

During my internship at ETFI, I contributed to the coordination of 3-day workshops for music students at Koninklijk Conservatorium Brussel. These workshops aimed to raise awareness of physicality and apply theatrical practices in various music genres, including classical music and jazz. The following outlines activities conducted during the workshop. We all have a notion of how we're perceived physically to some extent, but this perception might not be as concrete and precise as that of actors, who professionally study physical behaviors. Interdisciplinarity emerges from identifying gaps in our specializations and complementing them with knowledge from other disciplines.


Day 1: Concentration Training Games

・Moving objects in a given order collectively without oral communication
・Remembering smells and recognizing people and objects with them
・Quickly arranging participants based on physical characteristics such as hair length and shoe size, again without oral communication


Day 2: Participant Observation

・Singing and dancing exercises focusing on controlling physicality
・Impromptu performances with participants' specialized instruments


Day 3

・Impromptu performances within a decided framework
・Experimentation in spatial design, arranging positions of chairs, music stands, tables, mattresses, etc.
・Solo acting with instruments while incorporating a music stand into the setting (e.g., using a music stand as a prop like a baby, a selfie camera, or an interlocutor).
