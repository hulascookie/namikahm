Namika Hamahashi — Global Studies, Repetition, Connecting Dots

Rail Construction Project in Mombasa, Kenya

International Public Policy | Writing

2022

Final Essay for POLID414 International Development Policies in the French Language

 

After delving into the processes of policy-making and referencing works by authors such as James Ferguson, Michel Foucault, Peter M. Haas, and Barnett & Finnemore, I selected the Mombasa-Mariakani Road Dualling Project (MMRDP) as a case study for this essay, written in French. Coordinated by the Kenya National Highways Authority (KeNHA) and funded by three development banks, this project involves upgrading the A109 road between Mombasa and Mariakani in Kenya (approximately 42 km) from 2014 to 2020.




The essay primarily focuses on analyzing the intervention of the European Investment Bank (EIB) among the participating banks, which also include the African Development Bank (AfDB), Kreditanstalt für Wiederaufbau (KfW), and the EU-Africa Infrastructure Trust Fund. The aim is to analyze the project through three theoretical lenses: Bureaucracy (e.g., Michael Barnett, Martha Finnemore), Neopatrimonialism (e.g., Jean-François Médard), and the Transnational Advocacy Network (e.g., Margaret Keck, Kathryn Sikkink). Bureaucracy theory illustrates the behavior of European and African institutions, such as the EIB and KeNHA, in this particular development project. Neopatrimonialism theory examines the relationship between the Kenyan government and KeNHA. The concept of the Transnational Advocacy Network is important in understanding the role of NGOs, which, in this context, concern themselves with amplifying the voices of Kenyan citizens.




This essay highlights the authoritarian tendencies of both European and African stakeholders and underscores the vital role of information provided by international civil society in clarifying the project's reality. It is highly concerning that there have been almost no efforts by European and African bureaucracies to address various human rights violations, including forced evictions of local citizens, harassment against women, and deterioration in access to water and sanitation in the region due to this project. Future research could focus on applying the mentioned theories to interdisciplinary projects for an analysis of power dynamics.
